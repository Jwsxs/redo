echo is from greek mythology... she was a nymph (oread specifically) cursed to only repeat the last words spoken to her

## story
Zeus loved to get down on Earth and consort with Oreads - mountain nymphs, basically  
when Hera (Zeus' wife) started to find that weird, she came down when he was here to catch him live  
**Echo** was one of the Oreads Zeus loved to talk to, and, to distract Hera while her friends escaped, she started charmly chatting with her  
the myth goes on about when Hera realized it, she punished Echo with a curse that only allowed her to repeat the last words someone spoke to her  

there's a thing also with Narcissus, that they fell in love with each other, but she couldn't tell him about how she felt because of that curse, so my man started falling in love with himself...

## unix
`echo` is a command that dates 1969, original PDP-7's UNIX  
it's concept comes from a time when it clicked to them devs that you need to see what were you typing, so it'd "echoe'd" all your inputs.  
we're talking about the **teleprinter** / **teletyper** era

## explanation
literally, get the input, and print it out. as simple as that.
```c
char* input = argv[1];
printf("%s\n", input);
```
